# Investor Preference Questionnaire

## Investment Style

- Primary objective: Long-term capital appreciation with moderate risk.
- Time horizon: 10+ years.
- Typical holdings: Large-cap U.S. technology, consumer, and index funds.

## ESG and Stewardship Preferences

- ESG stance: Moderate. ESG issues matter when they are tied to long-term value, risk management, or transparency.
- Climate disclosure: Supportive. Favors consistent, decision-useful disclosure on emissions, transition planning, and climate risk oversight.
- Social issues: Supports reporting and oversight when proposals are focused, material, and actionable.
- Governance: Strong preference for board independence, clear accountability, and shareholder rights.

## Compensation Views

- Opposes excessive executive compensation, especially mega-grants, weak performance hurdles, or outsized dilution.
- Supports compensation plans when incentives are clearly tied to durable operating performance and shareholder returns.

## Transparency Priorities

- Values transparent reporting on political spending, lobbying, major regulatory risks, and sustainability claims.
- Prefers companies to explain why the board recommends a vote and how it evaluated shareholder concerns.

## Default Voting Lean

- Generally FOR routine items such as auditor ratification and uncontested directors, unless there are specific governance concerns.
- More skeptical of management proposals involving compensation or broad share authorization without a clear rationale.
- Open to shareholder proposals that request additional disclosure rather than rigid operational mandates.
